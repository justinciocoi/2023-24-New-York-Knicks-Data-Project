This page contains data visualizations made using MatLab. The data was extracted from the mySQL database using mySQL Workbench and analyzed using MatLab through data visualization via graph creation and analysis of statistical significance. Each graph below will have an associated description as well as a brief rundown of values that relate to statistical significance.

(Hover Images/Descriptions to Focus)


Double-Digit Scorers

This graph contains teams organized by wins and number of double digit scorers

  • A linear regression model was performed and a line of best fit was added to the data
  • While the R squared value is relatively low, a low p-value indicates that there is a statistical significance in this relationship
  • These findings suggest that teams with fewer double digit scorers perform to a higher standard during the NBA regular season
  • Some other factors that could impact this relationship include team chemistry, injury continuity, and age
  • It is important to note that a causal relationship was not found here, but the correlation indicates that this could affect other factors which, in turn, affect team success


Proportion of Double-Digit Scorers Over 20 PPG

This graph looks at the proportion of a team's double digit scorers who average more than 20 points per game

  • Again, a linear regression was performed and line of best fit added
  • This analysis yielded similar results to the previous one, with low R-squared and p-values
  • This suggests that teams with fewer, but higher volume scorers, may perform better
  • Once again this relationship is not causal, but the correlation likely indicates some relationship


Unique Players by Team

This graph looks at the number of unique players each team had throughout the course of the regular season

  • The total number of unique players represents the continuity of a team's roster throughout the season
  • This continuity might lead to team success, which led me to graph this relationship
  • As expected, teams with fewer unique players were likely to perform better than those with more
